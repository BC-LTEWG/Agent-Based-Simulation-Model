# Simulation of Trade Between Capitalist and Labor-Time Economies

## The Event-Based Simulation: A Dynamic Capitalist Economy Backend for the Trade Simulation
### Description / Overview

The event-based simulation implements a dynamic capitalist economy model designed to serve as the price- and supply-generating backend for an interface for trade between this capitalist economy and another economy based on labor-time accounting. Its purpose is to generate internally consistent trajectories of prices, outputs, supplies, labor usage rates, and value magnitudes under capitalist production relations, which can then be used by this trading system. It is built as a production-grounded and class-structured model. 

In addition to tracking prices and supplies, the simulation explicitly computes total reproduction demand at each time step, defined as intermediate production requirements plus worker and capitalist consumption demand. This allows reproduction feasibility and surplus conditions to be measured dynamically over time.

### Simulation Design

- Constructs a multi-commodity capitalist economy using a generated input–output matrix and labor coefficients
- Normalizes technical coefficients and the labor vector to ensure internally consistent production costs
- Initializes prices, supplies, outputs, wages, profits, and inventories from parameterized initial conditions
- Evolves the economy forward in discrete time by repeatedly applying a single event-based update step
- Records trajectories of prices, supplies, and related state variables at each time step
- Computes total reproduction demand at each time step as:
  - Intermediate production demand (A · q)
  - Worker consumption demand
  - Capitalist consumption demand
  - Total reproduction requirement = A·q + b + c
- Tracks both current total reproduction demand and equilibrium total reproduction demand for comparison with supply levels
- Detects equilibrium by identifying a time step with no change in prices and supplies relative to the previous step
- Records the continuous equilibrium time and extracts equilibrium price, supply, and total reproduction demand vectors as benchmarks
- Upon equilibrium detection, enables externally specified random events for a fixed post-equilibrium window (40 time steps)
- Applies the effects of enabled events directly to the economic state during simulation
- Computes the average reproduction gap (supply − demand)
- Exports full trajectory data with equilibrium annotations to CSV for downstream analysis, including:
  - Current total reproduction demand vectors
  - Equilibrium price, supply, and total reproduction demand vectors
  - Explicit equilibrium step indicators and equilibrium time annotation
- Produces visualizations of individual commodity prices, supplies, supply vs. reproduction demand, average supply vs. average reproduction demand, average reproduction gap, and aggregate averages, with equilibrium time explicitly marked in all plots

### Theoretical Frameworks Used

- Leontief input-output model
- Classical political economy
- Marxian value theory

## The Trade Interface

### Description / Overview

The trade interface implements a discrete-time simulation of exchange between a capitalist economy (CE) and a labor-time economy (LTE). Its purpose is to mediate trade between two economies operating under distinct value systems by identifying shortages and surpluses, constructing import and export demands, and executing trade based on value equivalence.

The interface consumes equilibrium and post-equilibrium data generated by the event-based capitalist simulation. Trade begins at the detected equilibrium of the CE simulation (treated as time step t = 0) and runs over a fixed 40-step post-equilibrium window.

The system integrates multiple empirical data sources to anchor value magnitudes and conversion parameters:

- World Bank – GDP per capita (PPP)  
  Used to measure the annual money value of output per person under purchasing power parity conditions.

- OECD – Average annual hours worked  
  Used to measure total annual labor time supplied by the working population.

- World Bank – PPP conversion factor  
  Because the United States is the reference country in the PPP system, international dollars equal U.S. dollars (conversion factor = 1), allowing all values to be expressed consistently in 2024 USD.

- U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (Producer Price Index – PPI)  
  Used to empirically calibrate relative commodity price ratios for the eight base product types.

- CME Group – 2024 Hot Rolled Coil (HRC) Steel Benchmark Price  
  Used to convert relative normalized prices into absolute monetary prices by anchoring the numéraire (common industrial metal) to an observed 2024 market price.

These data sources ensure that:

- The CE price system reflects empirically grounded relative production costs.
- Prices are expressed in absolute 2024 USD.
- The Monetary Expression of Labor Time (MELT) reflects real macroeconomic conditions.
- Labor-time values can be consistently converted into monetary equivalents.

The LTE is treated as a needs-driven system without endogenous price formation or accumulation dynamics. Trade effects currently modify monetary balances only; physical inventory adjustments are not yet implemented.

Trade is recomputed independently at each time step and does not assume persistent bilateral contracts.

### Simulation Design

#### Data Construction and Calibration

- CE equilibrium price, supply, and total reproduction demand vectors are loaded from the exported event-based simulation CSV file.
- CE relative prices (derived from the Leontief production structure and PPI-calibrated ratios) are converted into absolute 2024 USD prices by scaling with the 2024 U.S. steel benchmark price (938.07 USD per metric ton).
- CE quantities generated by the Leontief system are structurally real (proportional) units and are scaled by a fixed physical scaling factor to obtain magnitudes suitable for trade valuation.
- LTE labor-time values and fixed order sizes are loaded from external agent-based simulation data files.
- GDP per capita (PPP) and average annual hours worked are used to compute MELT.

#### Dynamic MELT Computation

At each time step, MELT is recomputed as:

MELT(t) = GDP_PPP_per_capita(t) / Average_Annual_Hours_Worked(t)

GDP and average hours worked are allowed to fluctuate stochastically within bounded ranges, producing a time-varying conversion rate between labor-time and money.

MELT represents the money value produced per labor-hour and is used to convert LTE labor-time values into monetary trade values.

#### Reproduction-Constrained Export Rule (CE)

For each CE commodity i, net excess supply is computed as:

net_excess_supply_i(t) = supply_i(t) − current_total_demand_i(t)

where current_total_demand_i(t) represents the reproduction requirement:

current_total_demand_i(t) = (A · q(t))_i + b_i(t) + c_i(t)

Interpretation:

- If net_excess_supply > 0, CE can export without disrupting reproduction.
- If net_excess_supply < 0, CE experiences a reproduction deficit and requires imports.
- If net_excess_supply = 0, CE is exactly reproducing.

Only surplus above reproduction demand is eligible for export.

#### LTE Shortage Generation

- At each time step, 0–5 LTE commodities are randomly selected to be missing.
- These goods are added to LTE import demand.
- LTE does not simulate endogenous production dynamics and does not accumulate surplus except for trade purposes.

#### Trade Value Construction

CE trade value per commodity is computed as:

value = absolute(physical_net_excess) × absolute_price

LTE trade value per commodity is computed as:

value = labor_time × MELT × order_size

Trade is computed in monetary value space, not in physical quantities.

#### Balanced Trade Rule

Total potential exports in each direction are aggregated:

Total CE to LTE value  
Total LTE to CE value  

Trade is strictly balanced:

trade_value = minimum(Total CE to LTE value, Total LTE to CE value)

No deficit financing or surplus imbalance is allowed.

Trade matching is aggregate and value-based; it does not solve a commodity-level matching or knapsack problem.

#### Monetary Accounting

Each economy maintains a total_currency variable.

When trade occurs:

- CE currency decreases by the value of imports and increases by the value of exports.
- LTE currency decreases by the value of imports and increases by the value of exports.

Because trade is balanced, net monetary effects cancel in aggregate, but balances are recorded and plotted over time.

#### Time Structure

- Trade operates only over the 40-step post-equilibrium window.
- At each time step:
  - CE state is updated from simulation time series.
  - MELT is recomputed.
  - LTE shortage is generated.
  - Import/export lists are rebuilt from scratch.
  - Trade value is computed and executed.
- No unresolved trade imbalances persist between time steps.
